# Key Findings

## Executive Summary
- Overall revenue declined despite stable website traffic and consistent average order value (AOV).
- Demand-side factors (traffic, pricing, AOV) were not the primary drivers of revenue loss.
- Payment gateway failures existed but remained relatively stable and insufficient to explain the decline.
- The largest revenue leakage occurred after successful payment, during order fulfillment.
- 20–30% of successfully paid orders failed to convert into delivered revenue.
- Returned orders were the single largest contributor to post-payment revenue loss.


## Detailed Findings

### 1. Orders Trend
- Month-over-month order volume showed minor fluctuations but no sustained downward trend.
- This indicates that customer demand and acquisition remained broadly stable.
- Order volume alone does not explain the observed revenue decline.


### 2. Average Order Value (AOV)
- AOV remained largely consistent across months.
- No structural decrease in pricing or basket size was observed.
- Revenue decline was therefore not driven by discounting, pricing strategy, or customer spend behavior.


### 3. Payment Failure Analysis
- Payment failure rates remained in the range of ~7–10% across months.
- While non-trivial, this rate was stable and did not spike during periods of revenue decline.
- Payment failures contributed to lost orders but were not the dominant revenue driver.


### 4. Post-Payment Fulfillment Issues
- Among successfully paid orders, a significant portion failed to reach delivery.
- Non-delivery rates ranged between approximately 20–30%, which is operationally high.
- This indicates breakdowns after payment completion rather than during checkout.


### 5. Return & Non-Delivery Breakdown
- Returned orders accounted for the majority of non-delivered paid orders.
- Pre-delivery cancellations formed the second-largest contributor.
- Other fulfillment failures (logistics delays, inventory mismatch) played a smaller role.
- Returns represent the most critical source of revenue leakage.


## Root Cause Conclusion
The revenue decline is primarily driven by post-payment operational failures rather than demand-side or monetization issues. High return rates and fulfillment inefficiencies prevent a substantial portion of successfully paid orders from converting into realized revenue. Improving post-payment execution presents the highest opportunity for revenue recovery.
