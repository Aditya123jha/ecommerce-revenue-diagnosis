## Revenue Leakage Summary

### Overview
This section quantifies the revenue impact of post-payment failures identified during the analysis. While customer demand, pricing, and payment systems remained largely stable, a significant portion of revenue was lost after successful payment due to fulfillment breakdowns.


### Revenue Funnel Breakdown

The high-level order-to-revenue funnel behaves as follows:

- Customers place orders and successfully complete payment.
- A substantial share of these paid orders fail to convert into delivered orders.
- Failed fulfillment results in revenue reversal through returns or cancellations.

This indicates that revenue leakage occurs **after** checkout, not before.


### Key Leakage Metrics

- **Paid Orders:** Stable month-over-month with no structural decline.
- **Non-Delivered Paid Orders:** Approximately **20–30%** of paid orders.
- **Primary Leakage Sources:**
  - Returned orders (largest contributor)
  - Pre-delivery cancellations (secondary contributor)

Even small improvements in fulfillment success would result in meaningful revenue recovery.


### Revenue Impact Interpretation

- Revenue loss is **not incremental**, but structural.
- Each failed delivery represents:
  - Lost gross merchandise value (GMV)
  - Additional logistics and handling costs
  - Reduced customer lifetime value (CLV)

At scale, a 20–30% post-payment failure rate creates persistent downward pressure on realized revenue, even when topline demand remains strong.


### Business Implication

The platform is effectively **leaking revenue after it has already been earned**.

This shifts the business focus away from:
- Marketing spend
- Pricing optimization
- Payment gateway tuning

And toward:
- Fulfillment reliability
- Returns reduction
- Operational execution


### Why This Matters

Improving fulfillment success has a **multiplicative impact**:
- Increases realized revenue
- Reduces operational waste
- Improves customer trust and repeat purchase behavior

Revenue recovery in this case is more cost-efficient than acquiring new customers.


### Conclusion

Revenue decline is driven by **post-payment revenue leakage**, not by lack of demand or monetization inefficiencies. Addressing fulfillment and returns represents the highest-leverage opportunity to reverse the revenue trend.
